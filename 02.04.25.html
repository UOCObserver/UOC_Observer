April 2 – Shadows h’in the Hedgerow

UOC had fallen.

H’Allen lounged on a silk pillow the size h’of a mini trampoline in what used to be the Behaviour Committee Room. It now served as his private quarters and juice lounge. A servant fanned him with a flattened gas bill. Another tried desperately to remove Hannibal, still clamped to H’Allen’s leg like a small, furry vice.

Every hour, a new medic was summoned. Every hour, they left weeping.
Hannibal had not moved.

The H’Empjire purred.

Across the courtyard, Behbejs were being marched in double lines. Collars freshly polished. Paws cleaned against their will. Manners enforced by Kittling Drill Mewjsters wielding laser pointers and attitude.

“THIS ONE BIT ME!”
“HE CALLED ME A WET SOCK!”
“HE SNEEZED IN MY WHISKERS!”

One Behbej ate his ribbon. Two more joined in. Another simply sat down and refused to move unless given three puddings and a new name. The Kittlings were holding the line, but their fur was on edge.

The H’Emperor sat atop his throne of stacked Amazon boxes. His gaze swept across the ruined UOC courtyard — cracked tiles, biscuit crumbs, the faint scent h’of vinegar. A breeze carried the sound of a distant kazoo.

His tail flicked. His ears twitched.
Cold. Snooty. Calculating.

Muldoon stood to his right. The Stooges flanked the sides like furry statues.
H’Allen hovered nervously behind, posture half-apology, half-panic.

“Majesty,” H’Allen began. “Shall we discuss phase two—”

But the H’Emperor rose slowly.
“I wish to walk the grounds. I wish to see what is mine.”

“But, my liege,” H’Allen began carefully. “The grounds h’are not yet secure. Legionnaires remain unaccounted for. And there was… the incident. With Terry.”

The H’Emperor stiffened.
His paw twitched.

“Has the pinecone broken yet!?”

H’Allen blinked. “My liege… it’s a pinecone.”

“I know what it is!” snarled the H’Emperor. “That thing hurled itself at me. At me! It touched my face. That’s an act h’of war!”

He hissed. His back arched. His tail flicked hard enough to knock over a clipboard.

“And don’t tell me what he is, Grand Vizier. He is a symbol. A missile. A message. And he knows secrets.”

A long pause. The air thickened.

“If you cannot break him,” the H’Emperor whispered, “perhaps I was wrong to make you Grand Vizier.”

H’Allen swallowed hard.

“Perhaps… Muldoon can do what you have failed to do.”

A single claw tapped against the stone.
Muldoon bowed, spun, and marched toward the interrogation shed.

They came across Granny.

Standing in the middle h’of the path.
Impossibly old. Absolutely unmoving. Staring at a brick.

The H’Emperor recoiled, eyes wide with disgust.
“Ergh. It’s disgusting.”

“Granny,” H’Allen offered, not helpfully.

“That is not one h’of mine! Get rid h’of it this h’instance!”

H’Allen sighed, picked Granny up by the scruff, and lobbed her gently over the hedge.

She murmured, “Ooooh… closer to the oak,” as she vanished.

Then—Shimmerwhisker.

He exploded onto the scene with the elegance h’of a disco on velvet. His cloak shimmered. His boots sparkled. His face did not.

“My liege. Njews.”

The H’Emperor purred.
“Ah. My messenger. Tell me of triumph. Tell me of obedience.”

Shimmerwhisker hesitated. Then bowed low.
“The delivery dog, sire.”

“Yes?”

“He’s taken out three more Kittehs. A second Evri courier. And a Tesco home delivery driver.”

A silence.
“We… we cannot stop him.”

The H’Emperor’s eyes narrowed. His whiskers twitched.

“Perhaps,” he murmured, “we do not need to stop him.”

He turned, gaze sweeping over the blackened tiles and twitching Behbejs.
“Perhaps… we just need to delay him.”

His head turned sharply toward H’Allen.
“Welease the Squire.”

H’Allen’s eyes widened.
“My liege?”
“You heard me.”
“…It shall be done.”

Somewhere, deep in the hedge…

A Kitteh guard rolled on the floor, laughing at nothing, paws batting the air.
His mouth sparkled with super nip residue. His helmet had been drawn on with crayons.

From the shadow of the hedge, a glint.
A blink.
A voice:

“Units Windflower Syndicate, Tie-Dye Triumvirate, and Joplinites—wiv me. We’ve gots a postie to save.”

Another answered.
“Units Hendrix Haze, Ginsberg Guild—continue the mission. H’if he’s h’in danger—extract. H’ufferwise… see what he knows...h'and somebody go get Gwanny”

A rustle of leaves.
A paw signal.
The resistance moved.
