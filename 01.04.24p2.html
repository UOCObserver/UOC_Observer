April 1 – A Chancinogion Special: The Roar h'of Redemption (or the Fall h'of it All)

Then, in the moment when all hope seemed lost, the sound of glass shattering cut through the chaos.

A crash. A slosh. A hiccup.

From behind the ruins of the biscuit cannon, staggering into view, came Nursy, bottle in hand. His eyeliner smeared, glowing faintly in the dimming light, his movements unsteady.

The battlefield paused. Even the H’Emperor turned his gaze toward Nursy, who was barely standing, bottle raised high.

With a drunken, slurred roar, Nursy yelled:

“NURSY 3:16 says I just whooped your… hick… ass.”

He lifted his paw with the bottle, his eyes glazed with a mixture of rage and drunken clarity.

He barked.

A mighty bark. A holy bark. A hiccupped bark.

For a brief moment, the clouds parted. A shaft of light poured through. Flowers bloomed, their petals trembling in the newfound hope. The Legionnaires—exhausted, broken—lifted their heads. The energy they had lost began to flicker again.

And then the H’Emperor laughed.

“You think we wouldn’t h’anticipate this?” he mocked, his voice thick with amusement.

He turned away, a lazy, dismissive gesture in his paw.

“Grand Vizier. Your Emperor needs you.”

In an instant, H’Allen acted.

A sharp blow.

A rolled-up final notice.

St Chance fell.

All heads turned.

It was H’Allen. Silent. Cloaked in tape. His expression distant, cold.

The bark died. The golden light faded. The battlefield froze.

Then shattered.

Legionnaires fled.

Daisies turned tail.

Hon tried to stop them—“STAND YOUR GROUND!”—but was struck across the muzzle by his own brother.

Nigel. Clipboard splintered. Eyes wet.

H’Allen dragged Nursy in a net toward the throne. Hannibal clung desperately to his leg, growling, biting. It made no difference.

The H’Emperor, watching with cold satisfaction, nodded.

“An excellent job, my Grand Vizier,” he said.

H’Allen said nothing.

The H’Emperor gestured. “Lock that one up too.”

Captured Legionnaires were lined up like cattle. They stood, beaten, broken.

The H’Emperor turned toward them with a cold, calculating look.

“Bring Hon to my tent. We shall discuss the surrender h’of UOC.”

Hon limped into the tent, defiance still burning in his eyes.

“Surrender?” he spat. “I’d rather eat Shimmerwhisker’s litter tray.”

The H’Emperor frowned.

“Gaol,” he said coldly. “No matter. The Grand Vizier shall handle a simple task like… admin.”

Hon’s eyes burned with fury as he was dragged away.

The H’Emperor climbed the steps to his throne. His gaze swept across the broken battlefield. His voice rang out, filling the air.

“Begin the Devşirme.”

Behbejs were herded forward. The smallest were whisked away. Collars were fitted. Grooming began.

UOC was broken.

Until—

A flick. A glint. A blur.

CRACK.

The H’Emperor recoiled. Muldoon shielded him. Guards rushed forward.

Gibbs retrieved the object.

A pinecone. With sunglasses. And a determined expression.

Terry.

Somewhere in the hedge…

Sergeant nodded.
